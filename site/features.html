Customize Missing e

Every one of Missing e's features are highly customizeable. You can turn off the parts you don't want, and leave others on!

The easiest way to change your settings is to click on the e at the top of any Tumblr dashboard page.

Bookmarker

Add bookmarks to posts on your dashboard so you can go back to them later!

Bookmarker adds a small bookmark button to posts, and a list of current bookmarks in your sidebar.

  • Hold down the key when clicking on a bookmark icon to create a new bookmark with a custom name.
  • Hold down the key when clicking on an existing bookmark to rename it.
  • Bookmarks can be dragged with your mouse to reorder them.

Dash Links to Tabs

Dash Links to Tabs forces all links in dashboard posts to open in a new tab. That way, you won't lose your place when you are scrolling in the dash.

Other links, such as the new post icons and the dashboard sidebar behave as you specify in the options.

Goto Dash Post

If you are on a tumblr post page and you want to jump directly to it on your dashboard so you can reply to it (assuming that the tumblr user's setup allows you to), Goto Dash Post is your best bet.

A "goto dash" control button is added to individual post pages for you to use.

Goto Dash Post
Magnifier

Add a small icon to photo posts on the dashboard to view the full-size images without ever leaving the dashboard. Magnifier will resize the images to fit on your screen, but you can click on the enlarged image to open it in a new tab.

This feature is not meant to replace the existing magnification feature on the Tumblr dash, only provide an alternative where the built-in feature cannot enlarge the image or does not display it at full resolution.

Magnifier can also add a icon to Tumblr avatars that, when clicked, allows you to view enlarged avatars!

Magnifier

Reblog Yourself

Enhance reblogging functionality to your own tumblr posts, allowing you to Reblog Yourself from any of your Tumblr blogs to any other blog on your account (including the same one).

Note: Chat and Ask posts are not normally rebloggable. Reblog Yourself may allow you to reblog them, but the formatting could look pretty bad.

Reply Replies

With Reply Replies, when you click on the icon of a notification in your dash, it'll open a new text post with that notification (and quoted reply, for post replies) in it, ready for you to comment on and post.

Although you can also reply to notes loaded by clicking on the "notes" button of a dashboard post, it is best if you have "Show notifications" turned on in your Tumblr preferences.

If you want to reply to multiple notifications in the same post, you can:

  1. Hold down the key and click on the icons of each of the notifications you want to reply to.
  2. Click (without ) on the icon of the last notification you want to include.
  3. All the notifications will appear in a single post in chronological order!

A tag can be automatically added to your post for each user to whom you are replying (and custom tags you specify in the settings)! You can also click the as... button at the top of your reply post to reply with a Photo post instead.

Reply Replies
Safe Dash

At work? Have kids about? Safe Dash will hide images in tumblr posts in case any of them are unsafe! Simply hover your mouse over the picture to reveal it. Turn Safe Dash on and off with a link in your dashboard sidebar! Turning Safe Dash off on this settings page will disable it completely.

Note: Safe Dash is not effective for posts on a user's tumblr page, only on dashboard-like pages (ie. dashboard, blog, likes, queue and drafts).

These images are hidden:

  • All image posts
  • All photoset thumbnails
  • Photosets that have been clicked (enlarged)
  • Inline images in other types of posts
  • Album art in audio posts
  • All video thumbnails
  • All full-size videos

These images are NOT hidden:

  • All tumblr-generated user avatars
  • Images enlarged by the 'Missing e' Magnifier feature.
